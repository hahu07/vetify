# Prohibited Sectors Reference

Source: AAOIFI Shari'a Standard No. 28 (Prohibited Business Activities) and CBN Non-Interest Financial Institutions Framework

## Absolutely Prohibited (Hard Reject — No Exception)

| Sector | Examples | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Alcohol | Production, distribution, wholesale, retail of alcoholic beverages | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Gambling & Betting | Casinos, sports betting shops, lottery operators, online gambling | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Tobacco | Cigarette manufacturing, tobacco distribution, vaping products | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Pork & Non-Halal Meat | Pig farming, pork processing, non-halal slaughterhouses | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Adult Entertainment | Pornography, adult clubs, escort services | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Conventional Interest Lending | Conventional banks (as borrowers), money lenders charging interest (riba) | AAOIFI Std. 28; CBN NIFI |
| Weapons & Arms | Arms manufacturing, weapons trading, military equipment (non-defensive) | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Drugs & Narcotics | Illegal drug production, trafficking, or distribution | AAOIFI Std. 28 |

## Prohibited Financing Structures (Murabahah-Specific)

| Structure | Why Prohibited | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| Refinancing existing conventional debt | Creates disguised riba (interest) | AAOIFI Std. 8, Clause 3/1 |
| Working capital with no specific asset | Murabahah requires identified asset | AAOIFI Std. 8, Clause 2/1 |
| Murabahah as cash advance | Cash-to-cash transaction is not permissible | AAOIFI Std. 8, Clause 2/3 |
| Speculative commodity trading (maysir) | Excessive uncertainty / gambling element | AAOIFI Std. 28 |
| Forward currency speculation | Gharar (excessive uncertainty) | AAOIFI Std. 28 |

## Mixed / Potentially Prohibited (Flag for Human Review)

| Sector | Risk | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| General grocery/supermarket | May stock alcohol or tobacco | >5% revenue from prohibited goods |
| Hospitality / restaurants | May serve alcohol | >5% revenue from alcohol |
| Pharmaceuticals | May include haram ingredients | Case-by-case KYB review |
| Entertainment venues | May involve music, mixed-gender events (varies by madhab) | Flag for Shariah officer |
| Insurance (takaful vs conventional) | Conventional insurance has riba/gharar | Flag — verify takaful only |
| Logistics & transport | If primary clients are prohibited businesses | Flag — assess client base |

| Sector | Notes |
|---|---|

| Agriculture & agro-processing | Crop farming, poultry (halal), fisheries |
| Textile & garments | Including fashion, tailoring, fabrics |

| Healthcare | Medical clinics, pharmacies (excluding haram products) |
| Construction & real estate | Building, renovation, property development |
| Education | Schools, tutoring, skills training |

| Renewable energy | Solar, wind — strongly encouraged in Islamic finance |

## CBN NIFI Additional Exclusions (Nigeria-Specific)

Per CBN Guidelines for Regulation and Supervision of Non-Interest Financial Institutions (2011):

- Financing must support real economic activity (no purely financial speculation)
- Cannot finance projects that violate Nigerian law or public order
- Must align with the objectives of Maqasid al-Shariah (protection of religion, life, intellect, lineage, wealth)
