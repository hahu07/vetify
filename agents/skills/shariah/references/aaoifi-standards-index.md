# AAOIFI Shari'a Standards Index

Relevant standards for Vetify's Murabahah financing platform.

## Primary Standards (Always Relevant)

| Standard | Title | Key Topics |
|---|---|---|
| **No. 8** | Murabahah to the Purchase Orderer | Asset identification, offer & acceptance, binding promise, profit disclosure, delivery, default |
| **No. 28** | Banking Services (Prohibited Activities) | Comprehensive list of prohibited business sectors and transactions |
| **No. 40** | Distribution of Profit in Mudarabah-Based Investment Accounts | Profit/loss sharing principles — relevant for understanding riba avoidance |

## Supporting Standards

| Standard | Title | Key Topics |
|---|---|---|
| **No. 1** | Trading in Currencies | Foreign exchange — relevant if financing involves FX |
| **No. 2** | Deferred Payment Sale | Instalment sales, mark-up rules — overlaps with Murabahah |
| **No. 5** | Guarantees | Third-party guarantees — relevant for collateral structure |
| **No. 9** | Ijarah & Ijarah Muntahia Bittamleek | Leasing — alternative to Murabahah in some cases |
| **No. 31** | Gharar — Stipulations & Conditions | Excessive uncertainty — relevant for ambiguous contracts |
| **No. 56** | Gold and its Trading Controls | Relevant for gold-backed financing |

## Key Clauses to Cite — AAOIFI Standard No. 8 (Murabahah)

| Clause | Content |
|---|---|
| 2/1 | The subject of a Murabahah sale must be a real, identified commodity or asset |
| 2/3 | Cash cannot be the subject of a Murabahah sale |
| 3/1 | The institution must actually own the commodity before selling it to the customer |
| 3/2 | The customer's promise to purchase is binding if the institution incurs cost |
| 4/1 | The profit margin must be explicitly disclosed — no hidden charges |
| 5/2 | Penalty for late payment may be charged only if donated to charity (not profit) |
| 6/1 | Rollover of Murabahah debt is not permissible (no compounding) |

## Key Clauses to Cite — AAOIFI Standard No. 28 (Prohibited Activities)

| Clause | Content |
|---|---|
| 3/1 | Prohibition of financing businesses primarily engaged in prohibited activities |
| 3/2 | Mixed businesses: permissible if prohibited revenue below acceptable threshold |
| 4/1 | Riba-based lending is absolutely prohibited |
| 4/3 | Speculative transactions with gharar are prohibited |

| Section | Content |
|---|---|
| 3.1 | Scope of permissible activities for licensed non-interest banks |
| 3.4 | Prohibited activities — mirrors AAOIFI with Nigerian regulatory additions |
| 4.2 | Shariah governance requirements — Shariah Advisory Committee mandatory |
| 5.1 | Murabahah financing permissibility conditions |
